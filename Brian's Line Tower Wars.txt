Brian's Line Tower Wars

Resolution: same as a vertical iPhone 16 pro max screen

The final product will be PvP intended for the Apple App Store. However, we'll need a means of testing it versus an AI opponent during production.

As in the game Clash Royale, the player will always see the same point of view: their towers and will appear at the bottom of the screen, their opponent's towers at the top. Their attackers will appear on the bottom of the screen and move upward. Their opponent's attackers will appear at top of the screen and move downward. 

#Visual Explanation

1. Along the lower left of the mockup is 5 rectangular boxes with different colored triangles. The boxes are representative of the clickable area in which the player can drag a tower, represented by triangles, into any of the 5 squares discussed in section 2 below. Each tower will have a cost, starting with the weakest tower found at the bottom (purple) on up to the highest cost (orange) at the top of this lower left area. To deploy a tower, the player must have sufficient resource (see section 3 below), then drag and drop it into one of the 5 squares in section 2. 
2. At the bottom center of the screen are the 5 positions in which a player can deploy towers. Once placed, the tower will use its unique defenses to prevent the opponent's attackers from reaching them. If an enemy attacker does reach the towers, the enemy gets a point. When a player has 20 points, the game is over and that player wins. 
3. There is only one resource which is represented in the mockup by a blue number 9 within a cross / diamond shape in the center right of the screen. Let's call it mana. Deploying a tower reduces mana. Deploying attackers reduces mana. Each has its own cost. Each player begins with 9 mana. 
4. Below the mana value cross, along the bottom right of the mockup is 5 rectangles, each containing an attacker monster the player can choose to send to attack the opponent. Clicking and dragging the attacker anywhere onto the center of the map will add it to queue for the next wave. Multiples of the same attacker can be deployed for a wave, the total numeric value for which should be shown masking the attacker's box with low opacity such that the attacker sprite and box are still easily visible to the player, and they know how many of that particular attacker will be sent at the opponent in the next wave. 
5. At the very bottom of the mockup is a bar representing a wave timer. When it is full, attackers begin to move towards the opponent's towers. The player can see the towers the opponent has crafted at the top of the screen; a flipped version of their own towers at the bottom. Each wave should have 10 seconds in between for the player to choose attackers and new tower deployments. 
6. In the center are two large grey triangles with red arrows showing you the path of each player's wave of attackers. Players will see their chosen attackers sprout from the bottom right side of the battlefield and move upward / leftward, and they will see the attackers their opponents chose appear from the top left of the battlefield, moving downward and rightward. 

Game Scope

Should v1 be 1v1 only (human vs AI) with online PvP deferred, or do you want local human vs human support in the first build too?: human vs AI is fine for initial builds as long as we're prepared to shift to online pvp seamlessly. 
Target platform first: browser desktop only?: yes, this is fine. 
Do you want the map to be a single fixed lane per player (classic line style) for v1?: yes
Win Condition: each attacker that makes it to the opponent's tower-line gains the attacking player one point. 20 points wins the game. 

How does a player lose: lives reach 0 when leaked creeps hit end?: a player wins at 20 points. 
Starting lives per player?: not applicable
Any match timer/sudden death, or only elimination?: match timer as per #visual explanation above. 
Economy: just one resource, mana. Each player begins with 9 mana, and subsequent waves add 9 + Wave Number (after Wave 1 it's 9+1, after Wave 2 it's 9+2, etc.)

Starting gold?: Mana, and 9. 
Gold income model: periodic tick, on creep kill, or both?: not applicable.
Income upgrades included in v1 or not?: not applicable. 
Waves and Sending

Should players manually send creeps to opponent, or auto-wave system, or both?: players manually choose creeps
For v1, how many creep types do you want at launch (recommend 3-5)?: 5
Is there a send cooldown/global pace limit to prevent spam?: no, the mana system should prevent spam
Towers

Roughly how many tower types in v1 (recommend 4-6)? 5
Tower upgrades in v1: none, 1 tier, or full multi-tier? No upgrades in v1
Any special abilities now, or only basic attack stats first? no special abilities in v1
Lane and Pathing

Grid/tile placement or free placement?: placements will be auto-generated by our game's AI
Fixed creep path with no mazing for v1, correct?: correct. 
Can towers block pathing, or should blocking be disallowed?: not applicable, see visual
Combat Rules

Damage model: simple single damage type for v1, or include armor/resist types now?: each tower will have its own range and damage value and attack rate. Creeps will simply have hitpoints and speed, their aim to reach the tower line and earn a point for the player. 
Targeting behavior: closest to exit, nearest, first-in-lane, or player-selectable?: closest to exit. 
Do you want projectile travel time, or instant hits for simplicity?: instant hits for simplicity in v1
AI Opponent (for testing)

Preferred AI behavior for v1: basic scripted (builds predefined towers + sends on timer) or slightly adaptive?: basic scripted for v1, keeping in mind we'll advance it in future iterations.

Any “must-do” AI actions you want (e.g., pressure early, save for mid game)? no, keep it balanced in v1
Meta / UX

Should we include a minimal lobby/setup screen (name, AI toggle, difficulty), or jump straight into match? straight to match in v1, keeping in mind we'll need this later. 
Visual style preference: very minimal placeholder art first, or themed Warcraft-inspired UI cues?: very minimal for now
